How to Swing Dance — Quick Guide

A friendly, practical walkthrough to get you stepping, connecting, and having fun.

Basic Steps & Progression

Start simple. Learn timing, posture, and the basic foot patterns. Once those feel natural, add connection, turns, and styling.

  • 1 — Find the beat (count 1–8) Start with the music: swing typically uses 8-count or 6-count rhythms. Tap your foot to the downbeat and count out loud: one-two-three-four, five-six-seven-eight (or 1&2, 3&4 for triples).
  • 2 — Posture & frame Stand relaxed but tall. Slight bend in knees, chest up, shoulders relaxed. Maintain a light, stable frame between partners — not stiff, not limp.
  • 3 — Leader/follower roles Leaders initiate movement and give clear non-verbal signals. Followers respond and add flavor. Roles are about responsibility, not rigidity.
  • 4 — Rock step Rock back on one foot then replace weight forward on the other (usually counts 1–2). Practice solo until the motion feels natural.
  • 5 — Triple step (chassé) A quick triple: step-step-step in a 1&2 rhythm. Common pattern is triple to the side or in place. Practice switching between rock step and triple step smoothly.
  • 6 — Combine: Basic 8-count pattern Common Lindy pattern: rock step (1–2), triple step (3&4), step back (5), step together (6), triple step (7&8). Work slowly, then speed up.
  • 7 — Connection & lead signals Leaders use body movement, torso rotation, and gentle guiding through the frame — not grabbing hands. Followers feel weight changes and respond.
  • 8 — Simple turns Add single turns: leader gives space and a gentle guiding hand; follower uses spotting and maintains balance. Practice one rotation at a time.
  • 9 — Timing & musicality Phrase your moves to musical phrases (8 or 16 bars). Accents and breaks in the music are great places for a pause or a playful move.
  • 10 — Footwork variety & styling Once comfortable, add kicks, syncopations, kicks, swivels, or Charleston moves. Keep balance and connection first — styling comes second.
  • 11 — Practice partner awareness Maintain safe distance, watch the floor, and communicate: a smile or quick verbal cue helps. Respect other dancers’ space.
  • 12 — Practice routines & social dancing Drill basics in slow practice, then play at social dances. Start with simple moves; aim to make your partner — and yourself — look and feel good.

Quick Practice Drill

2-minute loop:

  1. 30s: Stand and tap beat (count aloud)
  2. 30s: Rock step only
  3. 30s: Triple steps only
  4. 30s: Combine steps with weight changes
Repeat daily for fast improvement.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Pulling with the arms instead of guiding with the frame
  • Rushing triples — keep steady timing
  • Staring at feet — keep your head up and use peripheral vision
  • Not counting — counting anchors your timing

Final pep talk

Swing is about joy and connection. Start small, celebrate micro-wins, and remember: everyone was awkward at first. Smile, play some classic swing, and have fun.

Notes

If you want, replace the music you practice with classic Big Band or modern swing remixes at 100–140 BPM depending on comfort.

Tools & Resources

What to bring to a practice:

  • Comfortable shoes (smooth soles)
  • Water bottle
  • Open mind & sense of humor

Mini Tips

Follow with weight on the ball of the foot for quicker reaction.
